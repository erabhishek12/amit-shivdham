Situated on a sprawling 132 acres of land, AMIT stands as a beacon of technical education in Bihar. Located 15 kms West from Bounsi, Banka, our institute is easily accessible with Mandar Hill (Bounsi) being the nearest railway station.

We are committed to providing world-class education with state-of-the-art infrastructure, experienced faculty, and a focus on holistic development. Our vision is to create engineers who are not just technically sound but also socially responsible.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the admission process?
Students can apply online through our website or visit the campus for direct admission. Admissions are based on 10+2 marks and entrance exam scores (if applicable). Merit-based scholarships are also available.
What are the placement opportunities?
AMIT has an excellent track record with 100+ placement partners including top MNCs and startups. Our dedicated placement cell provides training, mock interviews, and career guidance to ensure students secure good packages.
Are hostel facilities available?
Yes, we offer separate hostel facilities for boys and girls with modern amenities including Wi-Fi, mess, recreation rooms, and 24/7 security. Hostel fees are separate from tuition fees.
What is the fee structure?
Fee structure varies by program. B.Tech programs range from ?70,000-?75,000 per annum, while BBA/BCA programs are ?50,000 per annum. Additional charges may apply for hostel, transport, and other facilities.
Is the college AICTE approved?
Yes, AMIT is fully AICTE approved and affiliated with Aryabhatta Knowledge University, Patna. All our programs are recognized and meet national education standards.

Anti-Ragging Cell

?? Zero Tolerance for Ragging

AMIT strictly prohibits ragging in any form on campus or outside. Ragging is a serious offense and is punishable under law. Our institution has established an Anti-Ragging Committee to ensure a safe and healthy environment for all students.

What constitutes ragging?

Any act of physical or mental abuse, teasing, or harassment of junior students by senior students is considered ragging. This includes verbal abuse, indecent gestures, causing psychological harm, or any form of intimidation.

Consequences:

Students found guilty of ragging will face strict disciplinary action including suspension, expulsion, and legal proceedings as per UGC regulations and Supreme Court directives.
